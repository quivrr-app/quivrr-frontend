Why Quivrr exists

Surfboard search should not feel like detective work.

Quivrr started because I could not find the surfboard I wanted anywhere online. I was jumping between surf shops, manufacturer websites, and listings trying to locate one specific board.

As a software engineer, I built tools to solve my own problem. Very quickly, it became obvious the problem was bigger than one board and one surfer.

Surfboards are spread across independent surf shops, manufacturer sites, and regional retailers. There is always a board sitting quietly on a rack somewhere that somebody would genuinely want if they knew it existed.

Independent surf shops should not miss that sale simply because their inventory cannot be discovered. Quivrr exists to connect surfers with boards, and boards with surfers.

The aim is simple: help surfers search one place instead of checking dozens of sites, make it easier for a retailer's stock to be found, and help the surf industry make better use of the boards already sitting in racks.

Best fit

What Quivrr is prioritising right now.

High-value live board stock

We are prioritising shops with strong live surfboard inventory, usable dimensions, clear product pages, and regional relevance.

Useful second-hand stock

Second-hand boards are in scope when the listing quality is clear enough to help a surfer understand what is actually available.

Not the focus

We are not prioritising rentals, lessons, repairs, accessories, or dealer-locator pages with no meaningful online board stock.
